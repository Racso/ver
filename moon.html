Compact Calendar Versioning

MoonVer

Day and time are instantly readable. Year and month are packed into X with a simple ÷20. Minute-level precision, MSI-compatible, designed for projects with a defined lifespan.

Formula

X = (year − 2025) × 20 + month  // 1–252
Y = day  // 1–31
Z = hour × 100 + minute  // 0–2359

Decoding X

X packs year and month using a ×20 multiplier. To decode, divide by 20: the quotient is the year offset, the remainder is the month. Dividing by 20 is easy — just divide by 2 and shift the decimal.

24 ÷ 20 = 1 remainder 4 → year 1 (2026), April
47 ÷ 20 = 2 remainder 7 → year 2 (2027), July
121 ÷ 20 = 6 remainder 1 → year 6 (2031), January
252 ÷ 20 = 12 remainder 12 → year 12 (2037), December

Properties

Monotonic sorting. X encodes year+month in a single increasing number. Y is the day. Z is the time. Standard version comparison produces chronological order.
12-year range. With epoch 2025, the scheme covers through December 2037 (X = 252). Sufficient for most product lifecycles.
Minute resolution. Z = HHmm directly, so every minute is distinguishable.
Full MSI compatibility. All components fit within 255.255.65535.

When to use MoonVer

MoonVer is for projects that need minute-level precision under MSI constraints. Day and time (Y and Z) are instantly readable — only X needs the ÷20 decode. That single division changes at most once a month, so in practice your team memorizes the current X value and only reads Y.Z.

If you don't need MSI, use SunVer. If you don't need minute precision, LightVer avoids the ÷20 entirely.
